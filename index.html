Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your first session.

Getting Started

Do you accept insurance?
We're out-of-network with most plans but provide a detailed superbill after each session that you can submit for reimbursement. Many PPO plans cover 50–80% of nutrition counseling.
What happens in the first session?
Your 60-minute intake covers your health history, current habits, labs (if available), and goals. You'll leave with a clear understanding of your personalized plan and next steps.
What should I bring to my first appointment?
Recent labs (if you have them), a list of current medications or supplements, and a general sense of your typical day of eating. Nothing else is required — we'll fill in the rest together.
Do you offer a free discovery call?
Yes — a complimentary 15-minute call lets us confirm we're the right fit before you commit to a full program. You can select this option on the booking page.

Logistics & Scheduling

Do you offer virtual appointments?
Yes — all services are available via secure telehealth video, in addition to in-person visits at our office. Most clients choose a mix of both.
What is your cancellation policy?
We ask for at least 24 hours' notice to reschedule or cancel a session without a fee, so we can offer the slot to another client.
Which states or regions do you serve?
In-person visits are available in the Portland, OR metro area. Telehealth is available to clients across most U.S. states — ask during your discovery call if you're unsure about yours.
How often will we meet?
Most programs start with weekly or biweekly sessions, tapering to monthly check-ins as habits solidify. Your cadence is adjusted to your goals and progress.

Programs & Results

How soon will I see results?
Every body is different, but most clients notice improved energy and habits within 2–4 weeks, with measurable clinical changes (labs, weight, symptoms) over 8–12 weeks.
Can you work with my doctor or specialist?
Absolutely — with your consent, we regularly coordinate care with physicians, endocrinologists, OBs, and therapists to ensure your plan aligns with your full care team.
Do you work with people who have food allergies or intolerances?
Yes — every meal plan and recommendation is built around your allergies, intolerances, and any medical restrictions from the very first session.
Is this a "diet," and will I have to give up my favorite foods?
No. Our approach is not restriction-based — we build flexible frameworks that include foods you enjoy, because plans you can't sustain don't create lasting change.

Still have a question?

Reach out directly — we typically respond within one business day.
